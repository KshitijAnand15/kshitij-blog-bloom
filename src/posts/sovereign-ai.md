My entire Twitter feed is filled with people talking about Sovereign AI post Fable 5 debacle. But I don’t understand what exactly Sovereign AI is and why we need it.

If Sovereign AI means Frontier Models built and consumed only by Indians - the deglobalized version, then I am bearish on it. If Sovereign AI means the Frontier Model built by India but consumed all around the world, then I have questions and hope.

### Who pays for it if it is only India?

- Pre-training a model requires a lot of capital. I see calls to GOI to fund our dream of Sovereign AI. GOI brings Babudom and bureaucracy into the picture. Middle Men in multiple layers. One can expect promises of ‘Ladla AI’ schemes to fund Sovereign AI, only if you vote for certain parties.
- VCs will fund our dream for AI. But like every business, VCs will look for returns. ROI on their investments. Sovereign AI will see the rise of AI agents and workflows. Even Service as a Software (SaaS of a different kind), is India Co. ready for the non-SaaS complex pricing models?
- Even if India Co. is ready, is there enough demand within to justify a huge capital inflow for training the models, which is just the first step? Everything comes down to ROI, and nobody is talking, but they should.

At last, we check all the boxes, but we will fall in the last ditch. VC and GOI pay up. India Co. starts working with complex business and pricing models. There is enough demand to justify the info.

But then you meet the final boss of selling anything in India -PROCUREMENT TEAMS.

It’s an animal that’s slower than an elephant. Babudom, but in a private organization. I can’t even imagine how their negotiations will go when the entire pricing of agentic workflows and agent inside their ecosystem depends on how complex ‘THEIR ECOSYSTEM’ is. I wish to keep myself updated on this development.

### AI by Bharat - for the entire world

That’s my hope. But we need to copy a lot of stuff that has gone right for these big frontier labs.

- This is what I’ve been reading about. Most AI tokens are being consumed for Coding. Claude Code ARR is $2.5B ARR. Codex sits at $2B ARR. The cursor went from 0 to hero because programmers with corporate cards paid to make their lives easy.[Source](https://t.co/IKYg6DidTe) If Sarvam is our answer to AI, they need to be known for coding first before anything. Period.
- We need CLIs for developers, a harness for wannabes like me who want to build agents so they can sit and chill. These use cases are minting money. Coding and agents are the AI market right now.
- Anthropic, OpenAI, Deepseek, and Quen do not market themselves as US or China-specific, or their branding has nothing to do with their country of origin. We need to do the same. Built by India, for the world. That is too cost-effective and reliable.

I am very hopeful for AI by Bharat for the entire world, and I am bullish on Sarvam as well. We need AI that works with our indigenous languages.

But we shouldn’t box ourselves into a pretty cage because we can’t look beyond nationalism.
